Differences Between Discriminative and Generative AI

    Discriminative AI focuses on classifying and distinguishing data into categories based on labeled training data, such as spam email filters.
    Generative AI creates new content by learning the underlying data distribution, producing outputs like text, images, audio, or video from prompts.

Core Models and Techniques in Generative AI

    Generative AI uses deep learning and artificial neural networks to simulate human-like creativity.
    Key generative models include Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs), Variational Autoencoders (VAEs), transformers, and diffusion models.

Historical Development and Applications

    Generative AI has evolved since the 1950s, with significant advances in the 1990s and 2010s due to neural networks and deep learning.
    Foundation models like large language models (LLMs) such as GPT series enable versatile applications across text, image, video, and code generation, impacting productivity and various industries.
