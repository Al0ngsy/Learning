Understanding Foundation Models and LLMs

    Foundation models are a new AI paradigm where a single model, trained on vast amounts of unstructured data, can be adapted to multiple tasks.
    Large language models like ChatGPT are a type of foundation model trained to generate text by predicting the next word in a sentence, enabling generative AI capabilities.

Advantages and Disadvantages of Foundation Models

    Advantages include superior performance due to extensive pre-training on terabytes of data and productivity gains by requiring less labeled data for specific tasks through tuning or prompting.
    Disadvantages involve high computational costs for training and inference, and trustworthiness challenges due to biases and unknown data sources in training datasets.

Applications and Innovations Across Domains

    Foundation models extend beyond language to vision (e.g., DALL·E 2), code generation (e.g., Copilot), and specialized fields like chemistry and climate science.
    IBM is actively innovating to improve the efficiency, trustworthiness, and applicability of foundation models in various business and scientific domains.
