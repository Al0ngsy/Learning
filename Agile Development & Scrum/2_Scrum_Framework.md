# Scrum Framework

Scrum is an Agile framework for developing and maintaining complex products through short, time-boxed iterations called sprints.

## Scrum Roles

- Product Owner: prioritizes value and manages the product backlog.
- Scrum Master: enables Scrum adoption and removes process impediments.
- Developers: build, test, and deliver increments each sprint.

## Scrum Events

- Sprint: a fixed time-box (commonly 1-4 weeks) to produce a usable increment.
- Sprint Planning: define sprint goal and select backlog items.
- Daily Scrum: inspect progress and adapt plan for next 24 hours.
- Sprint Review: demonstrate increment and gather stakeholder feedback.
- Sprint Retrospective: inspect team process and decide improvements.

## Scrum Artifacts

- Product Backlog: ordered list of desired product work.
- Sprint Backlog: selected work for the sprint plus delivery plan.
- Increment: completed, integrated output that meets Definition of Done.

## Definition of Done (DoD)

A shared quality bar that determines when work is complete. Typical DoD includes:

- Code implemented and peer-reviewed.
- Automated tests passing.
- Documentation and release notes updated.
- Security and performance checks completed as required.

## Example Sprint Flow

1. Product Owner refines backlog with team input.
2. Team performs sprint planning and commits to sprint goal.
3. Team executes work with daily scrums.
4. Team demonstrates increment in sprint review.
5. Team captures improvements in retrospective and applies them next sprint.
